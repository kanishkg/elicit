• There are 4 phases to the task: PRACTICE where you learn the controls to operate the robot, LEARN where you are taught about the robots thinking, and TEACH where you teach the robot by helping it complete the task.
  • Practice: Explore the controls and complete the task 3 times. To complete the task, move the robot arm (using W,A,S,D) with the gripper to the nut (rotate with C,V), pick the nut up (use SPACE to close the gripper) and move it to the peg. Insert the nut into the peg to finish the task. The image shows how the task is completed. You can press the Q key to reset the robot to its initial position.
  • Learn: You will then be shown 3 demonstrations from which the robot learned. Try and understand how the robot has learned to think.
  • Intervene: The robot will then try to complete the task 10 times. When you think that the robot is stuck or is not taking the best actions, you can intervene by pressing Z and taking over control. When you think that the robot can complete the task, you can switch control back to the robot by pressing Z.
    A panel with changing colors will give you feedback about the compatibility of your actions with the robots thinking. (red: bad, green: good).
  • Accepted: If your intervention is accepted, the robot will try to complete the task again.
  • Rejected: If your intervention is rejected, you'll be shown a replay of the demonstration showing parts of your intervention that were incompatible. You will also be shown an example from which the robot has been learning so that you understand how it has learned to think.
